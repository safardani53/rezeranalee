Marco Fabian: Early Life & Family

Marco Jhonfai Fabian de la Mora was born to his parents, Marco Antonio Fabian Vazquez and Adriana de la Mora.

Unfortunately, we could not find further information on Marco’s parents. Similarly, the details regarding his siblings also remain under wraps.

Moving on, Fabian grew up in the city of Guadalajara, which is famous for producing top football stars. Hence, ever since the 5 feet 7 midfielders learned to walk, he also started playing football.

Thereafter, be it day or night, Marco spent his time practicing football. Consequently, judging by the passion that Fabian had for the sport, his father decided to put him in Guadalajara’s youth academy in 1997.

After that, he spent the next decade rising through the ranks before making his senior team debut in 2007.

Marco Fabian: Career & Stats

Marco started his professional career with Guadalajara after graduating from the club’s academy.

Thereafter, the 30-year-old spent seven years at his hometown club, scoring a total of 46 goals in 229 appearances before moving to Cruz Azul on loan in 2014.

However, Fabian only spent one and a half years with Azul because of his loss of form. As a result, the Mexican playmaker native returned to his parent club, Guadalajara, in 2016.

Subsequently, returning to his hometown club sparked a fire inside Marco as he found his lost form and performed brilliantly for the next year and a half.

Consequently, Eintracht Frankfurt of German Bundesliga signed the talented midfielder for $4 million.

Promising Career

Although the 30-year-old started his Bundesliga career promisingly, he later saw his playing time fizzle out.

Hence, Marco decided that he needed to change his club to find more playing time. Subsequently, the Guadalajara native joined Philadelphia Union on a one-year deal.

During his one year at Philly, Fabian impressed many onlookers with his dazzling display of skills and raw pace. In fact, the 30-year-old scored eight goals in 25 games for his side, helping them reach the playoffs.

But, in a turn of events, at the end of the season, the Philadelphia Union declined to renew Marco’s contract. The result being, his astronomical wage that saw him earn $2.27 million during the 2019 season. 

After that, Fabian signed for Al-Sadd, who play in the Qatar Stars League for the 2020 season.

International Career

Talking about his international career, Marco has 43 caps for his national side, in which he has scored nine goals.

Moreover, during his time with El Tri, Fabian has played in the 2011 and 2013 Gold Cup, 2011 Copa America, 2017 Confederations Cup, and the 2014 and 2018 FIFA World Cup.

Similarly, the Guadalajara native had some beautiful moments in those tournaments. For instance, Fabian scored three goals and provided three assists during the 2013 Gold Cup.

He scored a goal against Germany in the 2017 Confederations Cup, which was later voted as the goal of the tournament.
