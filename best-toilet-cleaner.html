Best Toilet Cleaner 2019 | The Sun UK

Publish date: 2024-05-26

MOST of us find cleaning the toilet to be the job we dread the most around the house.

While we typically think of our toilet as the most germ-infested thing in the home, there are a surprising number of items that are far more germ-laden: washing machines, cutting boards and smartphones, to name a few.

At the same time, having a sparkling clean loo gives us a satisfaction like nothing else can. Perhaps it's to do with the fact that we Brits do spend an average of three hours plus on the loo every week, according to researchers.

All that toilet time certainly accounts for why we bulk-buy Aesop’s Post-Poo Drops to deodorise our toilets, and invest in blue- (or green or purple) tinged toilet tablets to give our loo water a pleasantly coloured shade.

There are a few different techniques for cleaning the toilet: you can get a hardcore, limescale-eliminating cleaner that you simply squirt in, a cleaner that requires you putting in some elbow grease, or a specialist cleaning product you drop into the cistern to sanitise the toilet for up to several months.

In addition to cleaning the bowl, you need to clean the outside of the toilet, the toilet seat, the hardware and even the floor around the toilet - and the more powerful the cleaning agent, the better.

While people have started avoiding bleach in other rooms of the house, most people won't object to using bleach to clean the toilet. Just remember to wear protective gloves to keep from getting irritated or burned when cleaning.
