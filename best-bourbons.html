Best Bourbons 2019 | The Sun UK

Publish date: 2024-05-23

LOVERS of liquor will normally rank bourbon up there with most top tier drinks.

It boasts a depth of flavour combined with a sweetness that will attract new drinkers and experts alike.

For it to be considered bourbon (and not just whiskey), the drink needs to be made with 51% corn. It also has to be aged in a charred oak barrel.

Due to these rules, Jack Daniel's is not actually a bourbon - it's a Tennessee Whiskey (otherwise it would have made it onto this list).

Another thing to look out for is age. If the whiskey hasn't been aged for over four years, they are required to put the age on the bottle. Most bottles without an age, then, are just over four-years-old - otherwise they'd brag about it on the bottle.

Bourbon is incredibly popular in the UK right now. It has a cheaper price tag than Scotch, with a less harsh and complex palette. The taste is more caramel than honey, for one thing. If you need help finding the right one for you, check out our guide of great bourbons you can buy in the UK.

1. Best cheap bourbon

Woodford Reserve Bourbon is incredibly cheap for the quality it delivers. Great for everyone but particularly people used to drinking Scotch, it trades sweetness for flavours of tobacco spice and dried vanilla, with a warming finish.

The great flavour should be no surprise, though: the whiskey is made on Kentucky's oldest distillery site. They've had a long time to perfect the recipe.

2. Best bourbon for beginners

The good thing about bourbon is it lacks the pretentiousness found with other drinks. Wild Turkey 101 is a well-made whiskey with a massive sweet kick, with notes of honey, caramel and brown sugar.

Its high proof (50.5% ABV) means it works incredibly well in cocktails, like an Old Fashioned.

  • (AD) Wild Turkey 101 Bourbon Whiskey, 70cl, for £25 on Amazon - buy here

3. Best smooth bourbon

Buffalo Trace is one of the most balanced of the cheaper bourbons. The whiskey is made from Kentucky and Indiana corn and aged in new American oak barrels. This process gives it equal parts sweet, oak and spice.

Coming in at 40% ABV, it's won't knock you off your feet like the others. Expect a much more smooth finish.

  • Buffalo Trace Bourbon Whiskey, 70cl, for £18 on Tesco - buy here

4. Best single barrel bourbon

Single barrel whiskey is basically what the name suggests: a drink that's been aged in a single barrel, rather than a blend of multiple. While this is more expensive to create, the bourbon develops a richer taste.

Four Roses Single Barrel (50% ABV) is a balanced, mellow sweet whiskey, with hints of stone fruits, spices and Morello cherries. The finish is long and delicate.

  • (AD) Four Roses Single Barrel Kentucky Straight Bourbon Whiskey, 70cl, for £42.01 on Amazon - buy here

5. Best aged whiskey

Eagle Rare's 10 year old bourbon is a complex drink, left to absorb the flavours of their carefully selected barrels. Having spent more time in the barrel, this will be less sweet and have a more earthy flavour.

Expect strong tones of oak, alongside candied almonds and rich cocoa.

  • (AD) Eagle Rare 10 Year Old Bourbon Whiskey, 70cl, for £36.49 - buy here 

6. Best small batch bourbon

Small batch whiskeys are made for connoisseurs due to their complex palette. They are normally created with a mash bill of under 200 bushels of grain and distilled into quantities of around 1000 gallons.

The 1792 Small Batch - named after the year Kentucky became a state - is a spicy drink, with a bigger rye profile.
