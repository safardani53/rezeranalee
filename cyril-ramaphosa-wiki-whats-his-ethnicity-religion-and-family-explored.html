Cyril Ramaphosa’s Religion: Is he a Christian?

Cyril Ramaphosa practices Christianity. The South African President is a devout Christian. The 70-year-old hails from a Christian background and follows Christian values. Ramaphosa was voted Student Christian Movement leader while attending Mphaphuli High School in Sibasa, Venda.

He graduated in 1971 and enrolled in law school at the University of the North (Turfloop) in Limpopo Province. Christianity is the most widely practiced religion in South Africa, with over 80% of the population being Christian.

Ethnicity Of Cyril Ramaphosa’s Parents And Family

Cyril Ramaphosa was born on November 17, 1952, in Soweto, Johannesburg, to Erdmuth and Samuel Ramaphosa. Similarly, Ramaphosa’s parents are of South African and Venda descent. Venda are South African Bantu people that live near the border with Zimbabwe. His father, retired police officer Samuel Ramaphosa, and mother, Erdmuth, were both housewives. Cyril came to fame as the secretary general of South Africa’s largest and most powerful labor union, the National Union of Mineworkers.

In 1991, he was chosen as ANC Secretary General by ANC President Nelson Mandela. Soon after, Ramaphosa was appointed as the ANC’s lead negotiator during the discussions that brought apartheid to an end. He was widely regarded as Nelson Mandela’s intended successor and was elected chairwoman of South Africa’s Constitutional Assembly after the country’s first democratic election in 1994. Cyril withdrew from politics in 1996 to pursue a business career. He rose to prominence as the owner of McDonald’s South Africa, a member of the board of Lonmin, and the chair of the board of MTN. Ramaphosa gained more success after returning to politics and was elected South Africa’s fifth president.
