CLIFFORD Smith Jr., known professionally as Method Man, rose to fame as a member of the Wu-Tang Clan.

He is also half of the hip-hop duo Method Man & Redman and has released several solo albums over the years.

Who is Method Man?

Born March 2, 1971, Method Man, 51, is known as an American rapper, songwriter, record producer, and actor.

He first rose to fame in the 1990s as a founding member of the hip-hop group Wu-Tang Clan, along with RZA, GZA, Ol' Dirty Bastard, Raekwon, Ghostface Killah, Inspectah Deck, U-God, and Masta Killa.

Since stepping into the entertainment industry, he has been a part of seven studio albums and has made over 100 guest appearances.

Method has also dabbled in acting, appearing in the films How High, Garden State, Method & Red, and The Wackness.

He has also had roles on several TV shows, including Power Book II: Ghost, Teenage Bounty Hunters, The Deuce, and The Last O.G.

Is Method Man married?

When Method is not in the studio, he can often be found at home with his family.

Since 2001, he has been married to Tamika Smith.

Since the two began dating, they have welcomed three children, including Sha, Rae, and Cheyenne.

What is Method Man's net worth?

Due to Method's successful career, he has been able to amass a fortune over the years.

As of June 2022, he has an estimated net worth of $14million, according to Celebrity Net Worth.

This number comes from his success with Wu-Tang Clan and Method Man & Redman, among other things.
