Adjoa Andoh is an English actress for film, television, theater and radio. She is known on the UK stage for her leading roles at the RSC, the National Theater, the Royal Court Theater and the Almeida Theater. She is also a familiar face on British television, most notably in two Doctor Who series with the role of mother companion Martha Francine Jones; 90 episodes of BBC Casualty's medical television series as staff nurse Colette Griffiths; and a year at BBC EastEnders. Andoh is the voice of the audiobook versions of Alexander McCall Smith's No. 1 Detective Agency novels; it won the title of "Audio Book of the Year" for the tenth novel in the series, Tea Time for the Tradition. She made her Hollywood debut in the fall of 2009, starring Brenda Mazibuko, Nelson Mandela's chief of staff, and Morgan Freeman as Mandela in Clint Eastwood's Invictus. Andoh has narrated numerous audiobooks including Auxiliary Justice and the follow-up books in the series.
