Glamorous Newmarket racegoers wear eye-catching dresses and bold fascinators as they enjoy Ladies Da

Publish date: 2024-03-30

COLOURFUL fascinators, daring stilettos and very eye-catching dresses, Newmarket racegoers arrived in style for Ladies Day. 

Revellers descended on the Suffolk course for the first day of the annual racing festival and did not hold back with their outfits. 

Women donned their fanciest frocks and unusual headpieces in a bid to be crowned the Style Awards Best Dressed Lady.

The stylish winner took home a number of swanky prizes, including a 12-month subscription to rental service The Devout, worth £708, and a House of Cavani voucher, worth £30.

As well as looking for creativity and originality, judges looked for someone who paid attention to the sustainability of their outfit.

And attendees certainly did not disappointed in their looks, oozing glamour from head to toe.

More on racing

Thankfully, racegoers were lucky enough to have good weather and the sun continued to shine on Friday too.

The racing event started with Britain’s oldest horse race, The Newmarket Town Plate, which had been run at Newmarket's Round Course since 1666.

It was launched by King Charles II, who insisted that it should be run “forever.”

Racegoers were treated to Frankie Dettori riding a big winner on the day after he made up with trainer John Gosden.

Dettori was a brilliant winner on Mighty Ulysses, the 11-10 favourite, in the Listed Edmondson Hall Solicitors Sir Henry Cecil Stakes at 4.10pm.
