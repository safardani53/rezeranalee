Net Worth & Income

Chris Paul made a tremendous net worth of $160 million from his NBA career as of 2023. Similarly, CP3 had his fair share of involvement in sponsorships and endorsements.

In 2018, he signed a four-year contract with the Houston Rockets worth almost $160 million. He had an annual average salary of $39.9 million.

The Rockets traded him with Russell Westbrook for the 2019 season. Per Clutchpoints, he was set to earn $85.5 million for the remaining two seasons.

Paul declined a $44.2 million contract with Phoenix Suns for the 2021-22 season on Aug 1, 2021.

After five days, he signed a 4 year $120 million contract with Phoenix, including $75 million guaranteed and an average annual salary of $30 million.

For the 2022-2023 season, he is getting $28.4 million; for 2023-24, on June 28, 2023, he will get $15.8 million fully guaranteed.

Forbes stated his salary as the second-highest in the whole NBA in the 2019/20 season. Also, he ranks as the 35th highest-paid athlete in the world.

Professional Career

Before he was drafted, the scouts noticed Chris when he scored 61 points in a senior season game.

It wasn’t coincidental but more symbolic as his grandfather was 61-year-old grandfather when he was murdered. Hence, Chris honored him by scoring one issue each year he lived.

As a result, Wake Forest University was ranked number one in the U.S. It was the first time in school history and lasted two weeks.

The New Orleans Hornets selected Paul fourth overall in the 2005 NBA Draft. Paul was chosen for the NBA All-Star Game in the 2007-2008 season.

But, Paul tore cartilage in his left knee in early February 2010. The surgery and medication kept him out for nearly a month. Hence, he missed the All-Star Game.

Eventually, Paul was moved to the Los Angeles Clippers before the 2011–12 season. And he quickly transformed the team into one of the league’s most dangerous squads.

Later, he re-signed with the Clippers on a long-term basis in 2013.

Paul’s outstanding play was discontinued when he suffered season-ending injuries. His teammate All-Star Blake Griffin also had the same fate.

Paul was moved to the Rockets the following season as he announced that he would not sign a contract extension with the Clippers in 2018.

Paul and star shooting guard James Harden led the Houston Rockets to the best record in the NBA in 2017–18 (65–17).

He was vital to the 2008 and 2012 Olympic gold-medal-winning teams.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Why did people start calling him CP3 as his nickname?

The CP in CP3 stands for Chris Paul's initials. His father and brother, who share the initials C.P., are CP1 and CP2, respectively. Hence, he inherited CP3 as the third member of the family with the shared initials.
