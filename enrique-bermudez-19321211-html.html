Enrique Bermúdez Varela, known as the Comandante 380, was a Nicaraguan soldier and rebel who founded and commanded the Nicaragua Contras. As a result, he became a global centerpiece in one of the most important conflicts of the Cold War. Bermúdez formed the largest Contra army in the war against the coalition government of the Sandinista coalition of Nicaragua consisting of Marxists, students, businessmen and religious groups, they received little support from the Union. Soviet Union, much more from Cuba and most democratic governments in Latin America like Costa Rica. and Venezuela. From 1979 until the end of the military conflict in 1990, Bermudez was the highest military commander of the Contras. In addition to being responsible for all Contras 'military operations, Bermúdez eventually helped manage Contras' transition to an opposition political party in the early 1990s, after a second election in Post-Somoza Nicaragua ended with the defeat of Sandinistas.
