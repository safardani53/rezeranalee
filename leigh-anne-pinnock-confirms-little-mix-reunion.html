Leigh-Anne Pinnock confirms Little Mix reunion two years after band split

Publish date: 2024-05-21

LEIGH-ANNE Pinnock has confirmed Little Mix will reunite - two years after they announced their hiatus.

The pop star took to the stage last weekend for Capital's Jingle Bell Ball as she threw herself into life as a solo performer.

Speaking before her incredible performance, Leigh-Anne exclusively told The Sun that it's just a matter of time before the girls team up again.

She said: "We Definitely will. But I mean, we've literally been solo for like two seconds and the other guys haven't even released yet, you know? So we have like time that we need to just be on our own. I guess."

Before then though, there's the matter of Perrie Edwards and Jade Thirlwall releasing their own solo efforts.

Breakaway member Jesy Nelson was the first to try solo music followed by Leigh-Anne, whose contemporary tracks have gone down a storm.

Despite crafting music in her own right, Leigh-Anne has no issues with teaming up with the girls on future releases.

"I feel like that's definitely an option," she said. "We always said that and joked about like featuring on each other’s albums, but I definitely feel like we're enjoying this time on our own you know to like grow and but yeah, I mean never say never."

Leigh-Anne addressed the group's turbulent final years in her new autobiography Believe.

She revealed all three members of Little Mix secretly sought therapy after Jesy Nelson’s decision to quit the group turned into a PR nightmare, with bitter online fan wars and countless reports of fighting.

The singer, 32, said Jesy’s abrupt exit from the band in December 2020 left her, Perrie Edwards, 30, and Jade Thirlwall, 30, “traumatised” and needing counselling.

Leigh-Anne writes: “It was a complex and painful thing to come to terms with. We all felt it, that pain and sadness. The whole thing felt like a weird break-up, and I wasn’t prepared for it.

“I wasn’t prepared for the intensity of those emotions. The whole thing was abrupt and sad and then it was messy, which made it even more painful.”
