The Sun on Sunday's Knit A Lot, Help A Tot appeal hits 10,000 blankets thanks to YOU

Publish date: 2024-08-13

THIS Christmas there will be premature and sick babies cuddling up to a warming blanket thanks to our generous readers.

You have donated more than 10,000 blankets since we joined forces last month with Bliss, the UK’s leading charity dedicated to babies born early or unwell, to launch our Knit A Lot, Help A Tot festive appeal.

After we asked for blankets for babies in neonatal wards, knitters from all over the country got out their needles and wool.

Bliss chief executive Caroline Lee-Davey said: “We’ve been thrilled with the fantastic response from The Sun on Sunday’s readers to our campaign.

“It has been amazing to see the care that has been put into knitting beautiful blankets for babies in neonatal care, as well as the generosity of those readers who have chosen to donate to Bliss.

“These blankets will be a treasured keepsake for many families whose baby is in hospital this Christmas. Thank you so much.”

The blankets you have sent in will go to mums just like Tammy Mullen, 34, who was in hospital in April at the height of the Covid pandemic with her newborn twin sons, Miles and Elijah.

Tammy, a sales assistant from Launceston, Cornwall, told how blankets were essential to keeping her tiny sons wrapped up warm.

She said: “The boys were too small for baby clothes at first, so it was vital for us to have blankets available in the neonatal unit.

"The hospital gave us some to use that people had knitted and donated, and we really appreciated them.”

Tammy had a caesarean at 34 weeks. Miles weighed 3lb 8oz and Elijah 4lb 4oz, so they were whisked straight to the neonatal unit, where they stayed for 31 days.

The new mum was discharged after 27 hours, but Covid restrictions meant she and partner Shayne, 34, a factory line operator, were not allowed to visit the boys together.

Tammy explained: “It was such a stressful time since only one of us could visit at a time. The hospital was 50 miles away and I couldn’t drive after the C-section, so Shayne had to wait for me outside.

How to make your blanket

Pattern for 24in x 30in baby blanket

YOU NEED: 250grams double knit yarn and 4mm needles (size 8). Cast on 128 stitches and knit 17 rows.

At the beginning and end of next and each following pattern rows you will knit 9 stitches for border to prevent curling up.

1st row: knit 9 (knit 10, purl 10) repeat to last 9 sts, knit.

2nd row: knit 9 (purl 10, knit 10) repeat to last 19 sts, purl 10, knit 9.
Repeat these rows 6 times.

15th row as 2nd row.

16th row as 1st row.

Repeat 15th and 16th rows 6 times to form the pattern (28 rows).

Repeat the above 28 rows of the pattern a further 6 times, the blanket should now measure approximately 28in.

Knit 17 rows then cast off.

Blankets started flooding into our storage hub at East London Textiles Ltd within days of the appeal being launched.

Now crate-loads are being carefully looked after by staff at The Big Yellow Self Storage Company, including Romford assistant store manager Jag Bhamber, 40, and we will start to distribute them this week.

Celebrities are backing our Knit A Lot, Help A Tot appeal too.

Where to send your finished blankets
