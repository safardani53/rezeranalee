Are mouse spiders in the UK and are they dangerous?

Publish date: 2024-08-12

VENOMOUS spiders are incredibly rare in the UK due to our climate.

With many people having phobias of our eight-legged friends, news of dangerous species are often received with fear.

Are mouse spiders in the UK?

This species of spider are more common in the UK nowadays, although the further north you go, you will not find so many living outside.

Should you live in the south, you are likely to see them in their natural habitat, living in burrows which are often covered by soil.

Their burrows usually go to a depth of around 30cms and have a hinged top of soil, known as a trapdoor.

However, in the north they are mainly found indoors.

It has been noted that the ones that are found living outdoors tend to be bigger.

In this country we have quite a limited number of spiders by comparison due to the climate.

When it comes to finding more spiders in your home, this tends to happen in seasons as the temperatures plummet outside and they go looking for warmth.

Should you have a fear of spiders, there are many measures you can take to try and limit the amount you have in your home.

What does a mouse spider bite look like?

Should you be unfortunate enough to be bitten by one, bites will resemble a pimple with a puss filled centre.

Around the bite will tend to be red, suggesting the area to be infected.

Although some people have reported reactions to the bites, these are rare.

There are few species of spider in this country capable of piercing the skin with a bite.

How large is a mouse spider?

Mouse spiders vary in length with a body anything from 10mm to 35mm.

They have bulbous heads and jaws which lead to them often being misidentified.

In the UK, we do not have many of the larger species of spiders which tend to require a much warmer climate to survive, although there still are some.

Are mouse spiders poisonous?

Although they do have a noticeable bite, the spiders don't pose any real threat to humans.

But they have been known to cause allergic reactions in people and can cause severe illness among children.

Spiders usually only bite people if they become trapped in your clothes or are handled roughly.

Anyone bitten by a mouse spider is advised to seek medical attention immediately.

Why is it called a mouse spider?

The spider first got its name due to its habit of living in burrows.

It was thought that, much like mice, it solely lived in such areas as the mouse.

The reality is that at 30cms in depth, the mouse spider burrows are nothing by comparison to its furry friend.

Some believe the name actually came from the grey coloured abdomen with tiny hairs that resemble mouse fur.
